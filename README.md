# Japanese Washi Craft Registry

This registry documents traditional Japanese washi papermaking techniques.  
Washi is one of Japan’s oldest crafts, produced through seasonal rhythms, specialized tools, and regional lineages. It is recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage.

## Purpose

The goal of this contract is to preserve essential washi techniques in a permanent and verifiable format.  
Papermaking knowledge is transmitted through family workshops and regional cooperatives, and many practices are vulnerable as artisan communities shrink.

By recording selected techniques on-chain, the registry protects cultural knowledge from loss or distortion and keeps the connection between technique, material, and ritual purpose.

## Why this contract matters

Washi is often reduced to “handmade paper,” but its real practice involves precise fiber preparation, controlled water flow, and specialized tools such as the sugeta frame.  
Each region — Echizen, Mino, Tosa — maintains distinct methods and materials.

This registry helps by:

- preserving verified information in a neutral and permanent format  
- keeping cultural context attached to each technique  
- offering a reference for artisans, researchers, and cultural institutions  
- preventing the simplification or misrepresentation of traditional papermaking  

## What makes this registry unique

The registry focuses on the craft process itself.  
It treats fiber cleaning, sheet formation, drying, and tool construction as cultural assets worth preserving.

The contract is intentionally minimal.  
It records only the essential information needed to identify and contextualize each technique.

## How it works

Each entry includes:

- region  
- workshop  
- technique name  
- material type  
- cultural note  
- ritual or traditional use  

A simple like and dislike system allows basic community feedback.
